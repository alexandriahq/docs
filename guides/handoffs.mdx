Handoffs are agent-ready packets of work.

When Ambient sees a task that a coding agent can help with, it prepares the relevant context and notifies you. You stay in control of what gets sent.

## How handoffs work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ambient learns the work">
    Ambient builds memory from your active work, capture settings, and integrations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ambient finds an opportunity">
    When a task looks ready for an agent, Ambient prepares a handoff.
  </Step>

  <Step title="You review it">
    Open the notification and check the goal, context, and suggested next steps.
  </Step>

  <Step title="You send it">
    Accept the handoff to open it in your connected agent, such as Codex or Claude Code.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Review a handoff

Before accepting a handoff, check:

- the goal is clear;
- the context is relevant;
- private or unrelated details are not included;
- the suggested agent is the right tool for the work.

If the handoff is close but not right, edit it before sending.

## Use handoffs well

Handoffs work best for extra work that can move without you holding every detail in your head.

Good examples include continuing a coding task, investigating a bug, writing a follow-up change, summarizing recent work, or preparing a concrete next step from a messy thread of activity.
