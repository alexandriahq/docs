Handoffs are agent-ready packets of work.

When the intent loop sees an intent with a clear goal, current state, enough evidence, and a concrete next step, it creates a pending handoff. You review it before anything opens in an agent.

## How handoffs work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ambient maintains intents">
    Ambient turns processed context chunks into open intents and keeps their summaries, goals, blockers, and status up to date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ambient checks context">
    The intent loop can use memory and connected read-only integrations before deciding whether the task is ready.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ambient creates steps">
    A handoff contains two to six ordered steps with concrete evidence, paths, names, constraints, and verification work.
  </Step>

  <Step title="You decide">
    Accept the handoff to open it in your configured agent, or deny it so Ambient does not keep asking on the same evidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Review a handoff

Before accepting a handoff, check:

- the goal is clear;
- the context is relevant;
- private or unrelated details are not included;
- each step is executable without asking you for missing information.

If a step is close but not right, add feedback on that step and let Ambient refine the handoff before accepting it.

You can also rate the handoff from 1 to 5 and leave overall feedback. Feedback upload is best-effort and does not block your local decision.

## Use handoffs well

Handoffs work best when Ambient has enough evidence for an agent to continue independently.

Good examples include continuing a coding task, investigating a bug, writing a follow-up change, researching a public blocker, summarizing recent work, or preparing a concrete next step from a messy thread of activity.

Coding handoffs need a project or repository path, the feature or bug goal, the relevant current behavior or failure state, and known constraints or decisions.

<Note>
  Agent setup is separate from app integrations. Codex and Claude Code receive handoffs through Ambient's read-only `ambient-context` skill. Apps like Todoist and Notion are integrations that Ambient can read while preparing the handoff.
</Note>
