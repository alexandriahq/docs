Ambient is currently invite-only. After you receive access, download Ambient App and complete the setup flow.

<Note>
  Look for an invite email from Alexandria or `access@workos.dev`. If you do not see it, check your spam folder.
</Note>

## Install

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up">
    Open your Ambient invite and create your account.

    Ambient uses WorkOS for sign-in, so the access email may come from `access@workos.dev`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download Ambient">
    Download Ambient App. Open it, then follow setup to install Bridge through Ambient.

    <a
      href="https://alexandria.so/download"
      style={{
        alignItems: "center",
        background: "#111111",
        borderRadius: "999px",
        color: "#ffffff",
        display: "inline-flex",
        fontWeight: 500,
        lineHeight: 1,
        marginBottom: "1.25rem",
        marginTop: "0.75rem",
        padding: "0.75rem 1.2rem",
        textDecoration: "none"
      }}
    >
      <span>Download Ambient</span>
    </a>

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="macOS">
        [Download Ambient App for macOS](https://api.alexandria.so/downloads/apps/ambient-app/alpha/darwin/arm64/latest.dmg)
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Windows">
        [Download Ambient App for Windows](https://api.alexandria.so/downloads/apps/ambient-app/alpha/win32/x64/latest.exe)
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install Ambient App">
    Open the Ambient App installer and launch Ambient.

    On macOS, move Ambient to **Applications** if prompted. On Windows, follow the installer prompts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install Bridge through Ambient">
    Ambient will guide you through installing Ambient Bridge.

    Bridge handles WorkOS sign-in, local pairing, delegated integration tokens, and the network path Ambient uses for remote inference.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Sign in">
    Sign in with the account from your invite.

    Keep Bridge running after sign-in. Ambient App pairs with Bridge locally.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose capture sources">
    Choose the local signals Ambient can use.

    Screenshots stay enabled so Ambient can understand visible work. You can also enable microphone, system audio, keyboard metadata, active app/window, and accessibility context.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Exclude private activity">
    Add apps, system-audio sources, and browser URL terms Ambient should skip.

    Music, Spotify, TV, and common private web surfaces are skipped by default. You can add more during onboarding or later in **Permissions**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Grant system access">
    Grant the OS permissions Ambient asks for.

    Depending on your capture choices, macOS may ask for screen recording, microphone, accessibility, input monitoring, and related access.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect an agent">
    Install Ambient's read-only `ambient-context` skill for Codex or Claude Code.

    This is separate from app integrations. Codex and Claude Code are agent toolchains that can open handoffs; integrations are private apps Ambient can read during intent review.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start working">
    You're done.

    Ambient runs in the background by default. Open **Feed** to see processed context chunks, or use the live recording overlay to inspect recent capture outputs.
  </Step>
</Steps>
